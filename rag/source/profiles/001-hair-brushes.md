## Business Model in Plain Terms

Manufacture and sell personal-use hair brushes (natural and synthetic bristle, wooden/plastic handles) from a small workshop. This is a domestic consumer goods play — low-skill assembly, locally sourceable raw materials, and a repeat-purchase customer base. The government profile targets 1,20,000 brushes per year sold at Rs. 15.63/unit (2003), scaling to a unit floor of Rs. 5 at the lower end of the range.

The business involves buying raw materials (bristles — synthetic or hog/boar, handle blanks — wood or ABS plastic), assembly/fitting, quality control, and wholesale distribution to retail channels. Minimal technical barrier, no heavy machinery.

## India Market Context (2026)

| Indicator | Data |
|---|---|
| India hair brush market size (2026) | ~USD 155 million (~Rs. 1,290 Cr) |
| CAGR (2023–2030) | 9% |
| Projected India market (2030) | USD 315 million (~Rs. 2,600 Cr) |
| India share of global market | 2.47% |
| Asia-Pacific growth driver | Fastest-growing region globally |

**Demand drivers:**
- Rising middle class disposable income (India GDP per capita CAGR ~6%)
- Young demographic: 600M+ people under 35
- Rapid expansion of domestic salon industry (grew 30%+ post-COVID)
- E-commerce accessibility — Flipkart/Amazon India enable SME-to-consumer direct channels
- Premiumisation trend: shift from comb-only households to multi-brush grooming kits

**Supply side:**
- Market dominated by unorganised/cottage sector players
- No single brand commanding >10% share in the low-price segment
- Strong OEM export potential to Middle East and South-East Asia from India manufacturers
- Raw material (synthetic bristle, ABS plastic) is entirely domestically available

**Competitive landscape:**
- Unorganised local manufacturers: ~60% of market
- Branded Indian players: Vega, Majestik, Roots Industries
- Imported (China): price competition in ₹50–₹200 segment
- White space: ₹20–₹60 rural/semi-urban segment largely unbranded — direct opportunity for MSME entry

**Verdict:** High-viability MSME opportunity as of 2026. Among the best risk-adjusted returns in this dataset. Suitable for fresh entrepreneurs with no prior manufacturing experience. E-commerce distribution via Meesho/Flipkart Samarth reduces the traditional retail barrier.

**Key risk:** Chinese import competition in the ₹30–₹80 price band. Mitigation: focus on ₹15–₹25 rural/semi-urban segment or ₹150–₹300 premium natural-bristle segment where Chinese competition is weaker.
